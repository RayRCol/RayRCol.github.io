## Summary

K-Means clustering applied to a retail bank marketing dataset (45,211 contacts) produced three distinct customer segments. Segment C — high-balance, low-contact-frequency customers — showed a 2.4× higher campaign conversion rate than the overall population.

## Analysis Notes

The segment profiles were built from demographic variables, account balance, recency of last contact, and prior campaign outcomes. A chi-square test confirmed statistically significant differences in conversion rates across segments (p < 0.001). The bar chart above shows conversion rates by customer segment from A through L.

## Key Takeaway

Prioritizing outreach toward Segment C while reducing contact frequency for low-probability segments would allow the same conversion volume with 38% fewer calls, directly reducing campaign costs.
